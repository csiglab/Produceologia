## Technique Space

> What is the space of culinary operational techniques?

| Category                      | Technique (Spanish)                                                | Description                                                                            |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Thermal – Dry Heat**        | Baking (**Horneado**)                                              | Cooking with dry heat inside an enclosed chamber; produces browning and firm textures. |
| **Thermal – Dry Heat**        | Roasting (**Asado**)                                               | High-temperature dry heat; ideal for meats and vegetables.                             |
| **Thermal – Dry Heat**        | Air Frying (**Freír al Aire / Freído por Aire**)                   | High-speed convection using minimal oil; crisp exterior, tender interior.              |
| **Thermal – Moist Heat**      | Boiling (**Hervido**)                                              | Full submersion in water at 100°C; softens and hydrates foods.                         |
| **Thermal – Moist Heat**      | Simmering (**Cocción a Fuego Lento / Hervor Suave**)               | Gentle cooking just below boiling; preserves structure and flavor.                     |
| **Thermal – Moist Heat**      | Steaming (**Cocción al Vapor**)                                    | Cooking with vaporized water; high nutrient preservation.                              |
| **Thermal – Mixed**           | Braising (**Braseado / Estofado Corto**)                           | Combination of searing and long moist cooking; breaks down tough fibers.               |
| **Thermal – Mixed**           | Stewing (**Estofado / Guisado**)                                   | Slow cooking fully submerged in liquid; produces tender results.                       |
| **Surface Contact Heat**      | Searing (**Sellado / Dorado Rápido**)                              | Intense surface browning for flavor development.                                       |
| **Surface Contact Heat**      | Pan Frying (**Salteado / Fritura en Sartén**)                      | Cooking in a thin layer of oil; between searing and deep frying.                       |
| **Surface Contact Heat**      | Grilling (**A la Parrilla**)                                       | Radiant heat from below; smoky, charred flavors.                                       |
| **Oil-Based Heat**            | Deep Frying (**Fritura Profunda**)                                 | Full immersion in hot oil for rapid crisping.                                          |
| **Mechanical – Cutting**      | Chopping (**Picar**)                                               | Reducing food size with a blade; increases surface area.                               |
| **Mechanical – Cutting**      | Slicing (**Rebanar / Cortar en Láminas**)                          | Producing uniform pieces for consistent cooking.                                       |
| **Mechanical – Mixing**       | Blending (**Licuar / Mezclar a Alta Velocidad**)                   | High-speed mixing to homogenize ingredients.                                           |
| **Mechanical – Structuring**  | Kneading (**Amasar**)                                              | Developing dough structure by pressure and folding.                                    |
| **Chemical – Flavoring**      | Marinating (**Marinar / Adobar**)                                  | Infusing flavor and softening tissues using acid, salt, or enzymes.                    |
| **Chemical – Preservation**   | Curing (**Curado / Salazón**)                                      | Applying salt, sugar, or nitrites to remove water and inhibit microbes.                |
| **Chemical – Transformation** | Fermentation (**Fermentación**)                                    | Microbial transformation producing new textures and flavors.                           |
| **Organizational**            | Mise en place (**Mise en place / Preparación Previa**)             | Organizing all ingredients and tools prior to cooking.                                 |
| **Safety & Hygiene**          | Sanitizing (**Sanitización / Desinfección**)                       | Removing contaminants via heat or chemicals.                                           |
| **Safety & Hygiene**          | Cross-Contamination Control (**Control de Contaminación Cruzada**) | Separating raw and cooked items to avoid biological risk.                              |

## QA

### (Cooking Technical Objects) What is the difference between a Personal Blender, Electrical Chopping, and Mechanical Chopping?

| **Category**               | **Personal Blender**                | **Electrical Chopping**                      | **Mechanical Chopping**                         |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Power Source**           | Electric motor (high RPM)           | Electric motor (low–medium RPM, high torque) | Manual / human force                            |
| **Primary Function**       | Liquefy, puree, emulsify            | Chop, mince, reduce solids                   | Chop, dice, slice with precision                |
| **Blade Speed**            | Very high (10,000–30,000+ RPM)      | Moderate (≈1,500–3,000 RPM)                  | Human-speed; variable                           |
| **Texture Output**         | Smooth liquids; homogeneous         | Even chopped solids; not liquid              | Controlled cuts; preserves structure            |
| **Best For**               | Smoothies, shakes, sauces, purées   | Vegetables, nuts, herbs, garlic              | Onions, vegetables, herbs, fine knife-like cuts |
| **Not Good For**           | Controlled chopping, uniform dices  | Smoothies or purées                          | High-volume work, liquid textures               |
| **Heat/ Oxidation**        | Medium (high RPM adds heat/air)     | Low                                          | Very low                                        |
| **Control Over Cut Size**  | Very low                            | Medium                                       | Very high                                       |
| **Typical Container Size** | 0.3–1 L                             | 0.3–1 L                                      | N/A (board or manual device)                    |
| **Examples**               | Nutribullet, Ninja Personal Blender | Mini chopper, food processor in small mode   | Knife, mandoline, mezzaluna, manual chopper     |
