### Ingrediente Prep  Technique(s)

| Technique | Description | Equipment Needed | Best For | Key Steps | Quality Indicators | Storage Notes |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|----------|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Blanching** | Brief boiling followed by ice water bath to partially cook, set color, and deactivate enzymes. | Pot, strainer, bowl with ice water | Vegetables (green beans, broccoli, asparagus), fruits (peaches for peeling) | 1. Boil water with salt<br>2. Add ingredient for 30sec-3min<br>3. Immediately transfer to ice bath<br>4. Drain thoroughly | Bright color, crisp-tender texture, easy peel removal | Refrigerate 3-5 days; freeze after blanching for long-term |
| **Dicing** | Cutting into uniform cubes of specific size. | Chef's knife, cutting board | Onions, carrots, potatoes, peppers | 1. Create flat surfaces (trim ends/sides)<br>2. Cut into planks, then sticks<br>3. Stack sticks and cut crosswise into cubes | Uniform size (brunoise: 1-3mm, small: 5mm, medium: 1cm, large: 2cm) | Varies by ingredient |
| **Julienne** | Cutting into thin, matchstick-like strips. | Chef's knife, mandoline (optional) | Carrots, zucchini, bell peppers, potatoes | 1. Cut into rectangular batons (2-3" long)<br>2. Slice batons into 1-3mm thick strips | Uniform thickness (1-3mm x 1-3mm x 2-3") | Use quickly or store in water for crunch |
| **Mincing** | Cutting into very small, irregular pieces. | Chef's knife, cutting board | Garlic, ginger, herbs, shallots | 1. Rough chop ingredient<br>2. Rock knife back and forth over pile<br>3. Occasionally scrape and redistribute | Fine, almost paste-like consistency without being puréed | Herbs: store in oil; garlic: use quickly or freeze |
| **Chiffonade** | Rolling leaves and slicing into thin ribbons. | Chef's knife, cutting board | Leafy herbs (basil, mint), greens (spinach, kale) | 1. Stack leaves, largest on bottom<br>2. Roll tightly into a cigar shape<br>3. Slice perpendicular to roll into thin strips | Uniform ribbons, minimal bruising (especially for basil) | Use immediately for herbs; greens store 2-3 days |
| **Tourné** | Cutting vegetables into football-shaped, seven-sided pieces. | Tourné knife (bird's beak) or paring knife | Potatoes, carrots, zucchini, turnips | 1. Cut into 2" cylinders<br>2. Trim ends to create football shape<br>3. Carve 7 curved sides from end to end | Uniform size/shape, smooth curved surfaces | Same as whole vegetable |
| **Concassé** | Peeling, seeding, and dicing tomatoes. | Chef's knife, paring knife, boiling water | Tomatoes for sauces, salsas, salads | 1. Score X on bottom, blanch 30sec<br>2. Ice bath, peel skin<br>3. Quarter, remove seeds/gel<br>4. Dice flesh | Clean dice, no skin/seeds | Use within 1-2 days |
| **Segmenting (Supremes)** | Removing citrus segments from membranes. | Parin knife, bowl | Oranges, grapefruits, lemons, limes | 1. Cut off ends, stand upright<br>2. Cut away peel and pith following fruit curve<br>3. Cut along membranes to release segments | Clean segments, no pith/membrane | Store in juice 2-3 days |
| **Butterflying** | Slicing through thickness without separating halves. | Chef's knife, cutting board | Meats (chicken breast), shrimp, fish fillets | 1. Place item flat<br>2. Slice horizontally almost through<br>3. Open like a book | Even thickness, still connected at one edge | Same as original ingredient |
| **Searing** | Browning surface at high heat to develop flavor. | Heavy skillet (cast iron/stainless), oil | Meats, fish, tofu, some vegetables | 1. Pat item completely dry<br>2. Heat pan until very hot<br>3. Add oil, then item<br>4. Don't move until browned | Deep brown crust, audible sizzle | Continue cooking or finish in oven |
| **Sweating** | Cooking slowly in fat without browning. | Sauté pan with lid, butter/oil | Onions, celery, carrots, peppers | 1. Cut vegetables small<br>2. Cook over low-med heat with lid<br>3. Stir occasionally until translucent | Soft, translucent, no color | Proceed to next cooking step |
| **Sautéing** | Quick cooking in small amount of fat over high heat. | Sauté pan/skillet, oil/butter | Vegetables, thin meats, shrimp | 1. Cut ingredients uniformly<br>2. Heat pan, add fat<br>3. Add ingredients, keep moving | Tender-crisp, slight browning | Serve immediately |
| **Deglazing** | Adding liquid to dissolve browned bits in pan. | Same pan used for searing/sautéing, liquid | All seared/sautéed items | 1. After removing main ingredient<br>2. Add wine/stock/vinegar to hot pan<br>3. Scrape bottom with wooden spoon | Liquid incorporates all browned bits | Base for sauces/gravies |
| **Marinating** | Soaking in seasoned liquid to flavor/tenderize. | Non-reactive container, marinade | Meats, poultry, fish, tofu, vegetables | 1. Combine acid/oil/seasonings<br>2. Submerge ingredient, refrigerate<br>3. Time varies (veg: 30min, meat: 2-24hr) | Penetrated flavor, tenderized texture | Discard used marinade or boil before reuse |
| **Brining** | Soaking in saltwater solution to add moisture/flavor. | Container large enough to submerge, salt, water | Poultry, pork, sometimes fish | 1. Dissolve salt (and sugar) in water<br>2. Submerge ingredient, refrigerate<br>3. Rinse, pat dry before cooking | Juicy result, seasoned throughout | Brine time: 1hr per pound |
| **Dry-Brining** | Applying salt directly and letting rest. | Salt, rack, refrigerator | Steaks, roasts, poultry, fish | 1. Salt generously on all surfaces<br>2. Place on rack in refrigerator<br>3. Rest 1hr-3 days (uncovered) | Dry surface, deeper flavor penetration | Creates own brine that reabsorbs |
| **Kneading** | Working dough to develop gluten. | Hands, counter, stand mixer | Bread, pizza dough, pasta | 1. Push with heel of hand<br>2. Fold, turn 90°, repeat<br>3. Until smooth/elastic | Windowpane test (stretches thin without tearing) | Rest before shaping |
| **Folding** | Gentle mixing to incorporate without deflating. | Spatula, bowl | Egg whites, whipped cream, batters | 1. Add lighter mixture to heavier<br>2. Cut down center, scrape bottom<br>3. Turn over, repeat until just combined | Uniform color, minimal volume loss | Proceed immediately |
| **Tempering** | Gradually raising temperature to prevent curdling. | Whisk, bowls | Eggs, chocolate, dairy | 1. Warm small amount of hot liquid<br>2. Slowly whisk into cool ingredient<br>3. Gradually combine mixtures | Smooth, no scrambled bits | Proceed with recipe |
| **Emulsifying** | Combining two immiscible liquids (oil/water). | Whisk, blender, food processor | Mayonnaise, vinaigrettes, sauces | 1. Start with base (egg yolk/mustard)<br>2. Add oil drop by drop while whisking<br>3. Increase to thin stream once stable | Thick, creamy, no separation | Store cool, shake/stir before use |
| **Infusing** | Steeping ingredients to extract flavor. | Saucepan, fine strainer | Oils, vinegars, syrups, creams | 1. Combine base and flavor agent<br>2. Heat gently (do not boil)<br>3. Steep, then strain | Clear flavor without particles | Store in clean container |
| **Clarifying** | Removing solids/milk solids from liquids. | Saucepan, cheesecloth/fine mesh | Butter, stocks, broths, consommé | 1. Melt/simmer liquid<br>2. Skim impurities from surface<br>3. Strain through multiple layers | Clear, golden liquid (butter); crystal clear (stock) | Ghee: shelf-stable; consommé: use quickly |
| **Reducing** | Boiling to evaporate water and concentrate flavor. | Wide saucepan, spoon | Sauces, soups, stocks, glazes | 1. Simmer uncovered<br>2. Skim impurities occasionally<br>3. Until desired consistency/flavor | Coats back of spoon, intensified flavor | Watch closely near end to prevent burning |
| **Rehydrating** | Restoring moisture to dried ingredients. | Bowl, hot/lukewarm water | Dried mushrooms, beans, fruits, chilies | 1. Cover with liquid<br>2. Soak until pliable (20min-overnight)<br>3. Reserve liquid if flavorful | Plump, tender, no hard centers | Use soaking liquid in cooking if clean |
| **Fermenting** | Using microbes to preserve and transform. | Jar, weight, airlock (optional) | Vegetables (sauerkraut), dairy (yogurt), grains (sourdough) | 1. Create environment (salt brine, starter)<br>2. Submerge below liquid<br>3. Store at correct temperature/time | Bubbles, tangy smell, sour flavor | Refrigerate to slow fermentation |
| **Pickling** | Preserving in acidic brine. | Jar, brine (vinegar/salt/water) | Vegetables, fruits, eggs | 1. Pack clean jar with ingredient<br>2. Heat brine, pour over<br>3. Seal, cool, refrigerate | Crisp texture, tangy flavor | Quick-pickles: 1hr-1week; canned: 1yr+ |
| **Curing** | Preserving with salt, sugar, nitrates. | Salt mixture, container, refrigerator | Meats (bacon, gravlax), fish, some vegetables | 1. Coat evenly with cure mixture<br>2. Refrigerate weighted (if needed)<br>3. Rinse, dry, sometimes smoke | Firm texture, concentrated flavor, preserved | Slice thinly, cook or serve as is |
| **Smoking** | Flavoring/preserving with smoke. | Smoker, wood chips, thermometer | Meats, fish, cheese, nuts | 1. Cure/brine if preserving<br>2. Smoke at low temp (cold) or cook (hot)<br>3. Until desired flavor/doneness | Smoky aroma, sometimes browned exterior | Refrigerate or freeze depending on method |
| **Grinding** | Reducing to small particles. | Grinder, food processor, mortar/pestle | Meats, spices, coffee, grains | 1. Cut into pieces that fit machine<br>2. Grind using appropriate plate/die<br>3. Keep equipment cold for meat | Uniform particle size | Use immediately or freeze (meat) |
| **Puréeing** | Making smooth, creamy mixture. | Blender, food processor, immersion blender | Soups, sauces, fruits, vegetables | 1. Cook until very tender<br>2. Blend in batches if needed<br>3. Strain for ultra-smooth | Velvety texture, no lumps | Can be frozen in portions |
| **Shucking** | Removing shell from mollusks. | Shucking knife, towel | Oysters, clams, scallops | 1. Hold with hinge facing you, towel for grip<br>2. Insert knife at hinge, twist to pop<br>3. Cut muscle, remove top shell | Clean separation, intact meat | Serve immediately on ice |
| **Filleting** | Removing bones from fish. | Fillet knife, cutting board | Whole fish | 1. Cut behind gills to head<br>2. Run knife along backbone from head to tail<br>3. Lift fillet, repeat other side | Clean, boneless fillet, minimal waste | Refrigerate on ice, use within 1-2 days |
| **Trussing** | Tying meat for even cooking. | Kitchen twine, poultry/roast | Whole chickens, roasts, stuffed meats | 1. Cut twine 3x length of item<br>2. Tie legs/wings close to body<br>3. Create even, compact shape | Uniform shape, secure but not too tight | Remove string before carving |
| **Scoring** | Making shallow cuts on surface. | Sharp knife, razor blade | Bread dough, meat fat, fish skin | 1. Use very sharp blade<br>2. Cut ¼" deep at even intervals<br>3. Follow pattern/function | Clean cuts, even depth | Allows steam/rendering/expansion |
| **Zesting** | Removing colored outer peel of citrus. | Microplane, zester, fine grater | Lemons, limes, oranges | 1. Wash and dry fruit<br>2. Grate only colored part (avoid white pith)<br>3. Use immediately or dry | Fine zest, no bitter pith | Freeze extra zest or dry for storage |
| **Segmenting Citrus** | Cutting fruit into membrane-free pieces. | Sharp paring knife, bowl | Oranges, grapefruit, pomelos | 1. Cut off ends, stand upright<br>2. Cut away peel and pith following curve<br>3. Cut along membrane to release each segment | Clean, pith-free segments | Store in juice in refrigerator |
| **Flambéing** | Adding alcohol and igniting. | Sauté pan, long match/lighter | Dishes with alcohol (crepes, steak) | 1. Heat alcohol slightly in separate pan<br>2. Add to hot food, tilt pan to ignite<br>3. Let flames die naturally | Brief flames, alcohol flavor remains without harshness | Exercise extreme caution |
| **Barding** | Wrapping meat with fat for moisture. | Fatback, bacon, caul fat | Lean meats, game birds, roasts | 1. Lay fat over top/sides of meat<br>2. Secure with twine<br>3. Remove last 30min for browning | Moist interior, crispy fat exterior | Can baste with rendered fat |
| **Larding** | Inserting fat strips into lean meat. | Larding needle, fat strips | Lean roasts, game meats | 1. Cut fat into thin strips<br>2. Thread through needle<br>3. Insert into meat at intervals | Even distribution of fat throughout | Keeps meat moist during long cooking |
| **Spherification** | Creating liquid-filled spheres. | Sodium alginate, calcium chloride, tools | Molecular gastronomy, cocktails | 1. Make alginate bath and calcium solution<br>2. Drop liquid into bath with syringe<br>3. Rinse spheres in water | Perfect spheres, thin membrane, liquid center | Serve immediately |
| **Sous Vide** | Precise temperature cooking in vacuum bag. | Sous vide immersion circulator, bags, sealer | Meats, fish, eggs, vegetables | 1. Season, vacuum seal<br>2. Submerge in water bath at exact temp<br>3. Cook for extended time, then sear | Perfect doneness throughout, extremely tender | Can hold at temp without overcooking |
