# Money Market

> A Money Market is a segment of the financial industry that deals with the trading of short-term debt instruments. These instruments typically have maturities of one year or less and are used by governments, financial institutions, and corporations to manage liquidity, short-term funding, and risk.

## Formulation

- **Market Type**: Primarily OTC, wholesale, short-term debt market.
- **Function**: Provides liquidity, funding, and risk management for institutions.
- **Accessibility**: Mostly institutional; retail access via money market funds.

## Product Space

| Product                       | Description                                                     | Typical Maturity         |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Treasury Bills (T-bills)      | Government-issued short-term debt                               | Few days to 1 year       |
| Commercial Paper              | Short-term unsecured debt issued by corporations                | 1 to 270 days            |
| Certificates of Deposit (CDs) | Bank-issued deposits with fixed term and interest               | 1 month to 1 year        |
| Repurchase Agreements (Repos) | Short-term loans backed by securities, usually government bonds | Overnight to a few weeks |
